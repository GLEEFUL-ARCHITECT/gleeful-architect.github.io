Well, this one's less of an actual development log and more of a meta-log of the
things I'm doing in preparation for development. The first few logs will likely be
similar, and the reason I'm being forced to do things this way is that I'm
coding the entire engine from scratch. For a while, there's going to be nothing
interesting going on from an outside point of view, so I decided the best way
to do things was to give you a look into my thought process.

I've wanted to make a game for a long time. It was actually one of my dreams when
I was very young; Since that time, most such dreams have died off, and yet this one
remains. All the effort I've put towards learning writing, art, programming, and
music was to further that goal, and it grows closer every day. But, to reach that
goal, I need to solidify the details of what I want to create, which I had avoided
doing for a long time.

So, I want to make an RPG. It's a format that has been explored by dozens, if not
hundreds of people before me, and I doubt that I could find many things to add to
it, but still, that's what I'm going to do.

Anyone who's followed my writing for a while should know that I'm a fan of
non-linear storytelling; I love to write stories that branch apart, stop, start,
and loop back upon themselves. Naturally, this means that any game I make, whether
intentionally or not, will eventually end up with some of these aspects. Thus, I
don't have much of a choice other than to lean into it. 

Every other game that's done this gives the player control of the nonlinearity
in some way, and this seems like an obvious storytelling choice, since it lines up
with their pre-existing ability to save and load the game. However, I feel that I
can do much more interesting things by exploring the inverse, a situation where the
player and their world is at the whim of a neutral entity.
